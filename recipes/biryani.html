How to make authentic Hyderabadi Biryani

Hyderabadi Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic Indian rice dish made with marinated meat and spices.

Hyderabadi chicken biryani is an aromatic, mouth watering and authentic Indian dish with succulent chicken in layers of fluffy rice, fragrant spices and fried onions. It is easier than most recipes while retaining the original taste and presented step by step. Cook like a native but with more ease!

Ingredients

  • Rice: Use long grained badmati rice. It has nice aroma and looks good after cooking.
  • Chicken: You can use boneless chicken or with bones.
  • Herbs - Coriander and mint leaves: Coriander leaves gives this dish a fresh aroma and mint leaves gives cooling effect to the body
  • Spices: Turmeric powder, red chilli powder and buryani masala powder.
  • Curd: Use fresh curd, it tastes sweet.
  • Ginger garlic paste.
  • Onions.
  • Lemon juice.
  • Green Chillis: You can skip adding green chillies to make it less spicy.
  • Salt: To taste.
  • Oil: You can use any cooking oil that does not have strong aroma.
  • Wholes Spices: Caraway seeds, green cardamom, black cardamom, cinnamon.
  • Saffron milk: a pinch of saffron soaked in 1/4 cup hot milk.
  • Kewra essence: Gives a nice aroma to the dish.
  • Ghee.

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken in yogurt, spices, and lemon juice for at least an hour.
  2. Fry sliced onions until golden brown and set aside.
  3. Cook basmati rice in boiling water until it's 70% done and set aside.
  4. In a large pot, add ghee, whole spices, and saut√© for a minute.
  5. Add the marinated chicken and cook until it's 80% done.
  6. Layer half of the rice on top of the chicken, followed by a layer of fried onions, chopped mint and coriander leaves, and saffron milk.
  7. Add the remaining rice on top of the layers and repeat with fried onions, mint, coriander leaves, and saffron milk.
  8. Cover the pot with a lid and seal the edges with dough or foil to make it airtight.
  9. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes or until the rice is fully cooked and the flavors have blended well.
  10. Serve hot with raita and salan (a spicy curry).
